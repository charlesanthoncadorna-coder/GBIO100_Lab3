Lab Overview

Explore the fascinating processes of pollination and double fertilization in flowering plants through interactive virtual experiments and data analysis.

This laboratory module connects the development of male and female gametophytes (from previous labs) to the culminating event of sexual reproduction in angiosperms: double fertilization. You will analyze provided data and media to understand the processes of pollen tube growth and double fertilization, integrating them into the complete plant life cycle.

Learning Objectives

Explain the process of pollen germination and pollen tube growth
Describe the path of pollen tubes through female reproductive tissues
Identify the key components and outcomes of double fertilization
Analyze experimental data related to pollen viability and germination
Integrate knowledge of plant reproduction into a complete life cycle

Case Study 1: The Fruitless Garden

Scenario: A gardener has tomato plants with abundant flowers but no fruit formation despite healthy plant growth and regular watering.

Task: Using your knowledge from this lab, identify two possible points of failure in the process from pollination to fertilization and suggest solutions.

Case Study 2: Seedless Watermelons

Scenario: Seedless watermelons are triploid (3n) plants that produce fruits without mature seeds.

Task: Propose a hypothesis for why triploid plants produce seedless fruits, based on the process of double fertilization.
